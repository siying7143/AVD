Scoring methodology

Explainable vulnerability prioritisation

The AVD score is a transparent heuristic used by your code to combine CVSS severity, exploitation evidence and Australian contextual relevance.

View score ranking
Formula

Final score calculation

final_score = min(10, 0.8 × NVD_base + exploitation_risk + AU_signal)

The formula keeps the result readable in the detail page. NVD CVSS is the technical baseline, exploitation evidence adds real-world urgency, and Australian source evidence increases local triage visibility.

1

NVD baseline

CVSS base score is multiplied by 0.8 so that exploit and local-context evidence can influence final priority.

2

Exploitation risk

CISA KEV and EPSS-derived evidence increase the score when exploitation is confirmed or statistically likely.

3

Australian signal

Australian source matches highlight records that may deserve visibility in an Australian cybersecurity context.

4

Priority level

The final score maps to Critical, High, Medium and Low labels used across the dashboard, table and detail page.

Reading the score

Priority interpretation

CriticalImmediate review, commonly high technical severity plus exploitation or strong contextual evidence.
HighPrioritise after critical items; meaningful technical impact or exploitation likelihood.
MediumTrack and remediate based on exposure, affected assets and operational context.
LowLower triage priority unless the affected system is unusually sensitive or exposed.
